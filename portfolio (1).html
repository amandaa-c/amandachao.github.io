The Philosophy

Research sits on shelves.
Stories change the world.

There is no shortage of evidence. There is a chronic shortage of people who can take that evidence and make others care about it — deeply enough to act on it. That is the work I do.

My background spans population health, neuroscience, pathophysiology, and child development. But my real expertise is translation. I take complex, peer-reviewed knowledge and ask: how do we mobilize this so it can actually inform what happens next?

Whether it's a white paper that lands on a policy-maker's desk, a data visualization that makes a finding undeniable, or a 1-pager distilling three years of dissertation research — I make knowledge usable, shareable, and impossible to set aside.

Knowledge Mobilization · Child Health

From 300-Page Dissertation to Policy Desk: Early Childhood Adversity Research

A doctoral researcher had spent four years producing ground-breaking findings on adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) and long-term neurological outcomes. The work was sitting in an institutional repository, largely unread. I condensed the research into a tiered knowledge translation package: a 2-page policy brief for government, a visual evidence summary for practitioners, and a public-facing 1-pager in plain language.

Deliverables Policy brief · Practitioner summary · 1-pager
Sector Child & Family Health
Timeline 3 weeks

Outcome

"The brief was shared directly with the provincial health ministry within two weeks of delivery. The researcher said it was the first time her work felt like it could actually change something."

Redesigning a Community Health Campaign That Wasn't Landing

A regional public health unit had data showing low uptake of a preventive health program despite significant outreach spend. I conducted a messaging audit, synthesized behavioral science literature, and rebuilt the campaign narrative around community identity and social proof rather than risk language. The redesigned materials were adopted province-wide.

Deliverables Messaging audit · Campaign framework · Asset brief
Sector Public Health Unit

Outcome

"Province-wide adoption following strong pilot results. The messaging reframe shifted the conversation from fear to agency."

Systematic Review · Neuroscience & Child Development

87 Studies synthesized into one actionable evidence framework

Synthesizing the Evidence on Sleep, Cognition & School-Age Learning

An education nonprofit needed a rigorous but readable review of the neuroscience evidence on sleep and cognitive performance to support a new policy initiative. I conducted a full systematic review and produced tiered outputs: a technical evidence appendix, an executive summary for school board trustees, and a plain-language guide for parents.

Deliverables Systematic review · Executive summary · Parent guide
Sector Education & Child Development

Outcome

"Distributed to 14 school districts. The parent guide was later featured in a national parenting publication."
